# Rewritten text

They laid off three departments in a single week. No town hall, no warning -- just calendar invites titled "Organizational Update" that everyone knew meant something worse. Some of these people had been there eight, ten years. One guy in logistics told me he'd just signed a lease on a new apartment the Friday before.

The severance was generous, I'll give them that. Six months plus outplacement coaching. But here's what no amount of transition assistance fixes: the look on someone's face when they're packing a desk they thought was permanent.

Was the restructuring necessary? Probably. The division had been bleeding money since 2024, and the board wasn't going to wait forever. That doesn't make it feel less brutal for the people on the wrong side of a spreadsheet.

The ones who stayed adapted faster than I expected -- though "adapted" might be too kind a word. They just... kept going. Fewer people, same deadlines, a kind of grim determination that gets mistaken for resilience. Leadership sent a thank-you email. It was fine. Nobody framed it.
