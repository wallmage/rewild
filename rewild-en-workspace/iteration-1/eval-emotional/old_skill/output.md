The layoffs hit hard. Hundreds of people lost their jobs in a single quarter, and no amount of corporate language softens that. The company offered severance and job placement help, which is the bare minimum when you upend someone's livelihood.

Was the restructuring necessary? Probably. The financials weren't sustainable, and everyone in the building knew it even before the announcement. That doesn't make it easier to watch coworkers pack boxes.

What struck me was how people handled it. Not "remarkable resilience" -- just ordinary humans figuring out their next move, helping each other update resumes, making introductions. The people who stayed carried heavier workloads without much acknowledgment for months.

Leadership sent the usual thank-you emails. Whether that lands as genuine or tone-deaf depends on what comes next -- whether the company actually follows through on the support it promised, or whether this all gets quietly forgotten by next quarter.
